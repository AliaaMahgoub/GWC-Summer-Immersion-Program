false solutions.

In order to maximize your impact, educate yourself and others on myths regarding the effects of and solutions to disposable packaging


  • "Make everything recyclable or compostable" -- Currently, much of what can be recycled or composted isn’t because the infrastructure requiredis not available in most parts of the world. Even when a fair amount of recyclable material is collected it might not be recycled in the end.

  • "Bioplastics" -- Most bioplastics, which can be made from as low as 20% plant material, require industrial processes to break down and many release toxic chemicals.

  • "Replace all the plastic in packaging with another material" -- Swapping throw-away packaging for different throw-away packaging is far from sustainable.

  • “waste-to-energy”, chemical recycling, or plastic-to-fuel projects are all dangerous false solutions

  • "Paper packaging" paper harvesting is responsible for a large share of deforestation. Paper fibers are not infinitely recyclable. They can only be recycled five to seven times. They need to be supplemented with virgin fibers.

real solutions.

here's what you can really do:

  • Prioritize no packaging/ naked products

  • Use less, both packaging and stuff in general.

  • Support innovations and markets that aim to make reusable and no packaging options.

  • Be a leader in business, government, and your community. Educate people of all ages on the dangers of disposable packaging.

  • Refute misconceptions with websites like EnvironmentalPaper

  • For more green options check out noissue's website!
